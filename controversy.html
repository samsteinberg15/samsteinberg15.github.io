The Wilpons

Madoff Investment Scandal
The Wilpon's were friends and partners with Bernie Madoff, a former market maker, who was the head of the largest Ponzi scheme in American History and defrauded thousands of investors. The Wilpon's were one of Madoff's largest investors, and have allegedly lost around half a billion dollars.
Meddling in Baseball Operations
The main role of the owner of a sports team is to hire the right people for front office roles, approve contracts and spend money. The primary role of General Manger is to trade, acquire, and find players and management. The GM is largely responsible for how well the team plays and operates, and will either get an extension or fired depending on the results. Moreover, The Manager of a baseball team is in charge of the day to day operations of the ball club. They create the lineups, strategies and make in game decisions.

Below are examples of the Wilpons getting "too" involved in baseball operations:

  • Jeff Wilpon did not dispute that he is in charge of day to day operations when asked.
  • Executives say the GM lacks autonomy and worn down by the Wilpons’ penchant for micromanaging via voluminous phone calls and meetings.
  • Jeff Wilpon forced injured Mets ace pitcher Pedro Martinez to start a meaningless game at the end of the 2005 season to sell more tickets. Wilpon allegedly said to Martinez "While I’m the boss here, you’re going to have to do what I say".
  • In 2005, Wilpon forced GM Omar Minaya to fire then Manager Willie Randolph at 3am eastern time while the team was in Los Angeles.
  • Peter Gammons, a sports media personality, said in 2009 that Jeff Wilpon is the real GM of the Mets, not Omar Minaya. Minaya is just there to take the heat.
  • Jeff Wilpon's reputation in the industry is that he is short tempered, tone deaf, a credit seeker, an accountability deflector, a micro manager, and bad at self awareness.

Three of the Worst Moves of the Decade

Yoenis Cespedes - 4 year $110 million

After the Detroit Tigers traded Cespedes to the Mets in the middle of 2015, he carried the team into the postseason, hitting .287 with 17 homeruns in just 57 games. After having a productive and relatively healthy 2016, he signed a 4 year $110 million contract to return. Cespedes has appeeared in 119 games since 2017, largely due to having surgery on both heels that caused him to be out for 8-10 months. While rehabbing a year after the surgery, Cespedes fractured his ankle on his ranch after getting "attacked" by a wild boar and stepping in a hole. Cespedes missed the entire 2019 season and may not even play in 2020.

More about Cespedes

Jason Bay - 4 year $66 million

When Jason Bay was a member of the Pittsburgh Pirates, he was consitently hitting around .280 with 30 homeruns, 100 RBI's and played nearly every game. The Pirates traded Bay to the Red Sox and he responded by hitting 36 homeruns, 119 RBI's, playing in the All Star Game and finishing 7th in MVP voting. The Mets signed Bay in the midst of his prime and thought they were getting one of the best and most reliable players in the MLB. In his 3 years with the Mets, Bay played in a just over 50% of the teams games, hit .234 with 26 homeruns and was released in 2012. He joins a long list of dissapointing players for a dissapointing franchise.

More about Bay

Robinson Cano, Edwin Diaz traded to the Mets

At the end of 2018, the Mets traded with the Mariners for Hall of Famer Robinson Cano and Star Closer Edwin Diaz while giving up top prospect Jarred Kelenic among others. The Mets were in the process of a rebuild and desperately needed a closer, but Seattle did not want to give up Diaz without getting rid of the expensive contract of the aging Cano. After several productive and healthy seasons with the Mariners, Cano was constantly injured, playing in 107 games. He also only hit .256, the lowest batting average of his career, to go with 13 homeruns and 39 RBIs, another career low. Diaz, who had a 1.96 ERA with 57 saves in 2018, pitched to the tune of 5.59 ERA and 26 saves and became a weakness in the Mets already infamously bad bullpen.
